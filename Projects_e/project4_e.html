Medical Malpractice Claims — Tail Risk Analytics & High-Cost Prediction

Built interactive dashboards and a predictive model to identify the small share of malpractice claims driving a disproportionate share of total payout.

Context

Medical malpractice payments represent a major financial burden in healthcare systems. In practice, a small subset of claims often drives a large share of total cost. Understanding where those high-cost cases originate is critical for risk management, insurance planning, and legal strategy.

This project analyzes historical malpractice payment data to uncover patterns behind financially extreme claims and to build an early-warning predictive model.

Business Problem

Organizations need to anticipate which claims may escalate into high-cost outcomes. Traditional reporting focuses on averages, which masks tail risk.

  • Identify early indicators of high-cost claims (tail risk)
  • Support proactive allocation of legal and risk resources
  • Improve visibility into specialties and contexts with elevated loss exposure

Analytical Approach

1) Data Preparation

  • Analyzed ~79,000 paid malpractice claims across severity, specialty, insurance type, attorney involvement, and demographics.
  • Ranked claims by payment amount and labeled the top 10% as High-Cost (binary tail-risk target).
  • Designed the modeling target to represent financially extreme cases without using payment amount as an input (prevents leakage).

2) Exploratory Dashboards

Built three Power BI dashboards to:

  • Establish baseline claim distribution and concentration patterns
  • Identify payment drivers across clinical and legal factors
  • Isolate and profile high-cost claims to understand tail-risk clustering

These exploratory views guided feature selection and informed how risk concentrates in specific clinical and legal contexts.

3) Predictive Modeling

  • Trained a logistic regression classifier to predict whether a claim falls into the High-Cost group.
  • Tuned the decision threshold to balance sensitivity and false alarms, selecting a cutoff around 0.60.

Results

  • Tail risk confirmed: Top 10% of claims account for ~43% of total paid amount.
  • Key risk signals: High-cost claims correlate strongly with elevated severity and private attorney involvement.
  • Concentration patterns: Certain specialty categories appear more frequently within the high-cost segment.
  • Strong model performance: ROC-AUC ≈ 0.93 (strong ranking capability).
  • Operationally useful threshold: At 0.60 cutoff, captures ~85% of high-cost claims while reducing false positives vs. default.

The model functions as an early-warning screening tool (not a final decision engine), helping risk teams prioritize review and intervention for claims likely to escalate.
