Synthetic Vehicle Damage Data Generation for Insurance AI

Designed a scalable AI pipeline to generate realistic vehicle damage images, helping insurance systems better identify and assess damage when real-world data is limited.

View Code Live / Report

Overview

This project addresses data scarcity in AI-driven vehicle damage assessment by generating high-quality synthetic car damage images. Using Stable Diffusion fine-tuned with LoRA and a Mixture of Experts (MoE) approach, we created a scalable pipeline that enables controlled generation of damage types and severity levels. The resulting synthetic data helps overcome limitations of real-world datasets and supports more reliable training of damage detection models

Business Problem

  • Limited and imbalanced training data: Real-world vehicle damage datasets are small, biased, and lack rare damage types, reducing the reliability of AI damage assessment models.
  • High cost of manual data collection: Collecting and labeling large volumes of damage images is expensive and time-consuming, especially for uncommon damage scenarios.
  • Barriers to scalable automation: Insufficient training data limits the ability to build accurate, scalable, and automated damage assessment systems for insurance claims.

Results

  • Successfully generated realistic synthetic images across multiple vehicle damage categories with controllable severity levels
  • Demonstrated that LoRA-based fine-tuning enables efficient specialization without full model retraining
  • Validated the feasibility of synthetic data as a supplement to real-world datasets using FID-based evaluation
  • Enabled broader coverage of rare damage scenarios, supporting more robust training of downstream damage detection models

Project Snapshot

Domain GEN AI / Computer Vision / Insurance Analytics
Tools Python, Stable Diffusion 1.5, LoRA, MoE, Hugging Face
Deliverable Synthetic image generation pipeline + evaluation

Approach

  • Curated damage-specific subsets from the CarDD dataset
  • Generated image–text pairs using a vision-language model
  • Fine-tuned Stable Diffusion 1.5 with LoRA adapters
  • Applied Mixture of Experts for controlled generation
  • Evaluated outputs using FID and visual inspection
