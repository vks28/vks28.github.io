Call Center Performance Analysis Dashboard

Built an interactive Power BI dashboard to monitor call volume, resolution efficiency, and customer experience, enabling data-driven staffing and service quality decisions.

View Code

Analytical Approach

The dashboard was designed around core call center performance dimensions to support operational and service quality decisions

  • Volume & Demand: Tracked total, answered, and abandoned calls to understand workload and capacity pressure
  • Service Quality & Efficiency: Monitored resolution rate, first-call resolution, talk duration, and speed to answer
  • Customer Experience: Incorporated customer satisfaction to evaluate experience beyond operational efficiency
  • Operational Dimensions: Enabled analysis by time (month, day, hour), agent, and call topic
  • Interactive Filtering: Allowed stakeholders to drill down dynamically and isolate root causes without separate reports

Business Questions Addressed

  • When are call volumes highest, and how does demand vary by hour and day?
  • How many calls are answered vs abandoned, and where are capacity gaps?
  • How effective is the call center at resolving issues on the first contact?
  • Which call topics drive longer handling times and higher workload?
  • How does agent performance vary across resolution, speed, and efficiency?

Key Findings & Insights

  • Nearly 19% of total calls were abandoned, indicating capacity constraints during peak periods.
  • Call volumes peak during business hours and vary by weekday, highlighting opportunities for staffing optimization.
  • Overall resolution rate (~73%) suggests moderate effectiveness, with room to reduce repeat calls.
  • Technical Support and Streaming topics show longer average handling times, signaling higher issue complexity.
  • Customer satisfaction (2.76 / 5) trails resolution rates, indicating that experience factors beyond resolution impact CSAT.
