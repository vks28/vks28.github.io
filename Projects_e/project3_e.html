Telecom Customer Churn & Retention Analysis

Analyzed customer churn and retention patterns using interactive Power BI dashboards to identify risk drivers, retention strengths, and opportunities for proactive customer management.

Analytical Approach

  • Designed dashboards around customer lifecycle stages: churn vs. retention
  • Analyzed customers across tenure, contract type, services, and pricing
  • Used segmentation by demographics, service adoption, and billing patterns
  • Enabled drill-down analysis to support root-cause identification and decision-making

Business Questions

  • Which customers are most likely to churn?
  • How do contract type, tenure, and services influence churn risk?
  • What distinguishes retained customers from churned customers?
  • Which customer segments represent the highest retention opportunity?
  • How do pricing and service adoption impact customer loyalty?

Dashboard 1 — Churn Analysis Insights

  • Month-to-month contracts drive the highest churn, far exceeding one-year and two-year contracts.
  • Customers with short tenure (< 1 year) show the highest churn risk, indicating early-lifecycle vulnerability.
  • CFiber optic customers churn at a significantly higher rate compared to DSL and non-internet users.
  • Churn is evenly distributed by gender, suggesting churn is driven by service and contract factors rather than demographics.
  • Customers using multiple services (streaming, phone, add-ons) still churn at high rates, highlighting experience or pricing issues rather than lack of engagement.

Dashboard 2 — Retention Analysis Insights

  • 73.5% of customers are retained, with the strongest retention seen among long-tenure customers.
  • Customers in the 6–12 month and 0–12 month tenure groups form the largest retained segments, indicating stabilization after early onboarding.
  • Two-year contract customers show stronger retention compared to shorter contract types.
  • Retained customers exhibit high adoption of core services, especially phone service and internet.
  • Retention is strongest in mid-range pricing tiers, while very low and very high monthly charges show weaker stability.

Combined Business Interpretation

  • Identifies high-risk churn segments early based on tenure and contract type.
  • Highlights retention levers, such as longer contracts and bundled services.
  • Supports targeted retention strategies for fiber-optic and short-tenure customers.
  • Enables telecom teams to move from reactive churn reporting to proactive retention planning.
